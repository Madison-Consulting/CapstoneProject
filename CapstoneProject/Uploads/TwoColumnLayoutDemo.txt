Here is a a list of the top ten tallest roller coaster as of last fall.
Bootstrap is based on a 12 column layout - Every row has 12 columns. Any div can have a class of row and the columns go inside. col-lg-6 is saying at the large resolution breakpoint, we want everything to take up six of those columns. Up until that point, the content will take up all of the columns, so the picture is the entire width of the browser and the text is below. View my site here to make better sense of what's going on.
https://hobbsna.github.io/roller-coaster-mania/tallest.html
Try playing around with the size of the browser to see what's going on here

When you see "mb-2" that's margin bottom 2.
See these links in the Bootstrap documentation for a better explanation of what I'm trying to say.
https://getbootstrap.com/docs/5.0/layout/grid/

This could also help (possibly) : https://getbootstrap.com/docs/5.0/utilities/spacing/
